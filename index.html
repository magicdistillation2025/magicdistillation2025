MagicDistillation

Pushing the Performance Limit of Few-Step Video Synthesis

Recently, open-source video diffusion models (VDMs), such as WanX, Magic141 and HunyuanVideo, have been scaled to over 10 billion parameters. These large-scale VDMs have demonstrated significant improvements over smaller-scale VDMs across multiple dimensions, including enhanced visual quality and more natural motion dynamics. However, these models still suffer from two major limitations: (1) High inference overhead, requiring approximately 10 minutes to synthesize a 28-step video on a single H100 GPU; (2) Depressing portrait video synthesis, such as unnatural facial expressions and movements. To address these limitations, we propose MagicDistillation, a novel step-distillation framework that can make large VDMs generate better portrait video synthesis even with significantly reduced inference overhead. The proposed MagicDistillation framework mainly includes three major contributions. First, we designed a novel weak-to-strong (W2S) distribution matching method that leverages one weak LoRA and one strong LoRA together to achieve faster generation and robust distillation of large VDMs, while conventional distribution matching distillation (DMD) usually generates uncontrollable videos and fails to distill large VDMs. Second, to further improve generation quality and generalization of the distilled few-step generator, we propose a data-centric regularization framework, which encourages the generative data distribution to closely approximate a designed mixed domain regularization dataset with a generative data regularization technique. Third, our extensive experimental results on portrait video synthesis demonstrate that MagicDistillation can significantly reduce inference overhead and improve portrait video quality, outperforming Euler, LCM, and DMD baselines. Notably, in terms of both visualization and I2V-VBench evaluations, to the best of our knowledge, we are the first to achieve 4-step portrait video synthesis that can beat SOTA open-source VDMs, including WanX-I2V (14B) and HunyuanVideo-I2V (13B), which even require 50-step generation and 25$\times$ higher inference overhead.
